CASE_ID: 994
DIFFICULTY: step1
SYSTEM: infectious_disease
DIAGNOSIS:
Infective endocarditis (acute; Staphylococcus aureus)
ALIASES:
acute infective endocarditis
S. aureus endocarditis
VIGNETTE_LINES:
- A 31-year-old man with a history of IV drug use presents with fever and malaise for 4 days.
- He reports pleuritic chest pain and shortness of breath.
- Exam shows a new holosystolic murmur best heard at the left lower sternal border.
- Multiple tender nodules are noted on the pads of the fingers.
- Blood cultures are positive within 12 hours.
- Echocardiogram shows mobile vegetations on a cardiac valve.
TEACHING_POINTS:
- Acute endocarditis is most commonly caused by Staphylococcus aureus.
- IVDU classically involves right-sided valves (tricuspid) with septic pulmonary emboli.
- Tender fingertip nodules suggest Osler nodes (immune complex phenomenon).

CASE_ID: 995
DIFFICULTY: step1
SYSTEM: gastroenterology
DIAGNOSIS:
Amebic liver abscess (Entamoeba histolytica)
ALIASES:
Entamoeba histolytica liver abscess
amebiasis
VIGNETTE_LINES:
- A 27-year-old man presents with fever and right upper quadrant pain.
- He recently traveled to rural Mexico and had several weeks of intermittent bloody diarrhea that resolved.
- Exam reveals hepatomegaly and marked tenderness over the liver.
- Labs show elevated alkaline phosphatase and mild leukocytosis.
- Ultrasound shows a solitary hypoechoic liver lesion.
- Aspiration yields thick brown fluid with no bacteria on Gram stain.
TEACHING_POINTS:
- Entamoeba histolytica can cause liver abscess after intestinal infection.
- “Anchovy paste” (brown) aspirate is classic and often sterile on Gram stain.
- Treat with metronidazole followed by a luminal agent (e.g., paromomycin).

CASE_ID: 996
DIFFICULTY: step1
SYSTEM: neurology
DIAGNOSIS:
Myasthenia gravis
ALIASES:
MG
anti-acetylcholine receptor antibodies
VIGNETTE_LINES:
- A 29-year-old woman presents with intermittent double vision and droopy eyelids worse at night.
- She reports that symptoms improve after resting.
- Neuro exam shows fatigable weakness with sustained upward gaze.
- Reflexes and sensation are normal.
- Ice pack test over the eyelid improves ptosis.
- CT chest reveals an anterior mediastinal mass.
TEACHING_POINTS:
- Myasthenia gravis causes fatigable weakness due to antibodies against postsynaptic ACh receptors.
- Ocular symptoms (ptosis/diplopia) are common early findings.
- Associated with thymoma; treat with pyridostigmine and immunosuppression when needed.

CASE_ID: 997
DIFFICULTY: step1
SYSTEM: endocrinology
DIAGNOSIS:
Pheochromocytoma
ALIASES:
catecholamine-secreting tumor
VIGNETTE_LINES:
- A 44-year-old man has episodic headaches, palpitations, and sweating.
- Episodes last 10–20 minutes and occur several times per week.
- BP in clinic is 190/110 mmHg, but normal between episodes.
- He has unintentional weight loss and tremor.
- Urinary metanephrines are elevated.
- CT abdomen shows an adrenal mass.
TEACHING_POINTS:
- Pheochromocytoma causes episodic catecholamine surges: headache, sweating, tachycardia.
- Diagnosis: plasma or urine metanephrines.
- Pre-op management: alpha-blockade first (phenoxybenzamine) before beta-blockade.

CASE_ID: 998
DIFFICULTY: step1
SYSTEM: heme_onc
DIAGNOSIS:
Acute promyelocytic leukemia (APL)
ALIASES:
APL
t(15;17)
VIGNETTE_LINES:
- A 36-year-old woman presents with fatigue, gingival bleeding, and easy bruising.
- Labs show anemia and thrombocytopenia.
- Peripheral smear shows abnormal promyelocytes with multiple Auer rods.
- PT and aPTT are prolonged and fibrinogen is low.
- She develops diffuse oozing from venipuncture sites.
- Cytogenetics reveal a translocation involving chromosomes 15 and 17.
TEACHING_POINTS:
- APL is associated with t(15;17) and can trigger DIC.
- Auer rods in promyelocytes are a classic clue.
- Treat with all-trans retinoic acid (ATRA) ± arsenic trioxide to promote differentiation.

CASE_ID: 999
DIFFICULTY: step1
SYSTEM: biochemistry
DIAGNOSIS:
Ornithine transcarbamylase (OTC) deficiency
ALIASES:
OTC deficiency
urea cycle disorder
VIGNETTE_LINES:
- A 2-day-old male develops poor feeding, vomiting, and lethargy.
- He becomes tachypneic without signs of lung disease.
- Labs show markedly elevated ammonia with normal anion gap and no metabolic acidosis.
- BUN is low.
- Urine orotic acid is elevated.
- Symptoms worsen after protein-containing feeds.
TEACHING_POINTS:
- OTC deficiency is X-linked and causes hyperammonemia without metabolic acidosis.
- Elevated orotic acid distinguishes OTC deficiency from CPS I deficiency.
- Management includes protein restriction and ammonia-scavenging therapies.

CASE_ID: 001
DIFFICULTY: dental
SYSTEM: periodontics

DIAGNOSIS:
Acute Necrotizing Ulcerative Gingivitis

ALIASES:
ANUG
trench mouth
necrotizing gingivitis
Vincent's disease

VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. A 22-year-old male college student presents with severe gum pain and bleeding that started two days ago.
2. He has been under significant academic stress, sleeping poorly, and smoking heavily.
3. Examination reveals punched-out ulceration of the interdental papillae with a gray pseudomembrane and fetid halitosis.
4. There is no lymphadenopathy and the patient is afebrile, though he reports general malaise.
5. Periapical radiographs show no bony involvement or alveolar bone loss.
6. The clinical triad of interdental necrosis, spontaneous bleeding, and characteristic foul odor confirms the diagnosis, and the patient is started on debridement and metronidazole.

TEACHING_POINTS:
- ANUG is characterized by the triad of pain, bleeding, and necrosis of the interdental papillae.
- Predisposing factors include stress, smoking, poor sleep, and immunosuppression.
- Treatment involves gentle debridement, oral hygiene instruction, and metronidazole for severe cases.

CASE_ID: 002
DIFFICULTY: dental
SYSTEM: endodontics

DIAGNOSIS:
Irreversible Pulpitis

ALIASES:
pulpitis
acute pulpitis

VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. A 35-year-old woman presents with spontaneous throbbing tooth pain that woke her from sleep last night.
2. She has a history of a large filling placed in the same tooth two years ago.
3. The affected lower molar is exquisitely tender to percussion and the pain lingers for over 30 seconds after a cold stimulus is removed.
4. There is no swelling, trismus, or fever suggesting spread beyond the pulp.
5. Periapical radiograph shows a deep restoration in close proximity to the pulp chamber without periapical radiolucency.
6. Electric pulp testing confirms a hyperresponsive vital pulp with prolonged painful response, and root canal therapy is initiated given the irreversible nature of the inflammation.

TEACHING_POINTS:
- Irreversible pulpitis is distinguished from reversible pulpitis by pain that lingers after the stimulus is removed.
- Spontaneous or nocturnal pain strongly suggests irreversible pulpal inflammation.
- Treatment is root canal therapy or extraction; the pulp cannot heal.

CASE_ID: 003
DIFFICULTY: dental
SYSTEM: oral surgery

DIAGNOSIS:
Pericoronitis

ALIASES:
wisdom tooth infection
third molar pericoronitis

VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. An 19-year-old man presents with pain and swelling in the back of his lower jaw for three days.
2. He reports difficulty opening his mouth fully and pain when swallowing.
3. Examination reveals erythema and swelling of the soft tissue flap overlying a partially erupted lower third molar with purulent exudate on probing.
4. He is afebrile with mild trismus but no fascial space swelling.
5. Panoramic radiograph shows a partially erupted lower left third molar with inadequate space for full eruption.
6. Irrigation under the operculum yields purulent debris, and the patient is started on amoxicillin with a referral for third molar extraction once the acute infection resolves.

TEACHING_POINTS:
- Pericoronitis is inflammation of the soft tissue around a partially erupted tooth, most commonly the lower third molar.
- The operculum traps food and bacteria, predisposing to infection.
- Acute management includes irrigation and antibiotics; definitive treatment is extraction of the offending tooth.

CASE_ID: 004
DIFFICULTY: dental
SYSTEM: oral medicine

DIAGNOSIS:
Oral Candidiasis

ALIASES:
oral thrush
pseudomembranous candidiasis
denture stomatitis

VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. A 67-year-old woman presents with a sore mouth and a white coating on her tongue and inner cheeks for two weeks.
2. She recently completed a course of broad-spectrum antibiotics for a urinary tract infection and wears a full upper denture.
3. Examination reveals creamy white plaques on the buccal mucosa and dorsal tongue that wipe off easily leaving a red erythematous base.
4. There is no cervical lymphadenopathy and the patient is otherwise well.
5. No imaging is indicated and clinical findings are sufficient for diagnosis.
6. A potassium hydroxide preparation of scraped material reveals pseudohyphae and budding yeast forms consistent with Candida albicans, and the patient responds to topical nystatin rinse.

TEACHING_POINTS:
- Oral candidiasis is the most common oral fungal infection and is often precipitated by antibiotics, dentures, or immunosuppression.
- Pseudomembranous candidiasis features white plaques that wipe off, distinguishing it from leukoplakia.
- KOH preparation revealing pseudohyphae confirms the diagnosis.

CASE_ID: 005
DIFFICULTY: dental
SYSTEM: oral pathology

DIAGNOSIS:
Ameloblastoma

ALIASES:
mandibular ameloblastoma

VIGNETTE_LINES:
1. A 34-year-old man presents with a slowly expanding painless swelling of his lower jaw noticed over the past year.
2. He denies pain, trismus, or numbness, and has no history of trauma or prior dental pathology.
3. Examination reveals a firm buccal cortical expansion in the posterior mandible with facial asymmetry.
4. There is no cervical lymphadenopathy and overlying mucosa appears normal.
5. Panoramic radiograph shows a multilocular radiolucency with a soap bubble appearance in the posterior mandible causing root resorption of adjacent teeth.
6. Incisional biopsy reveals islands of odontogenic epithelium with peripheral palisading and reverse polarity of nuclei, confirming ameloblastoma requiring wide surgical resection.

TEACHING_POINTS:
- Ameloblastoma is the most common odontogenic tumor and is locally aggressive with high recurrence if not widely excised.
- It classically presents as a multilocular soap bubble or honeycomb radiolucency in the posterior mandible.
- Histology shows ameloblast-like cells with peripheral palisading and nuclear reverse polarity.
